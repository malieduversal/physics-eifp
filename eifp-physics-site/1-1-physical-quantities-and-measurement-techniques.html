1.1 Physical quantities and measurement techniques

Measure length, volume and time accurately, then learn what makes a quantity a scalar or a vector.

2Notes

Core

Measuring length

The SI unit of length is the metre (m). A ruler is precise enough for small objects, usually to the nearest millimetre. For longer distances, a tape measure or trundle wheel is more practical.

UnitIn metres
1 kilometre (km)1000 m
1 centimetre (cm)0.01 m
1 millimetre (mm)0.001 m
Reading a ruler correctly: if the object doesn't start exactly at the zero mark, subtract the starting reading from the final reading — don't assume the object's length equals wherever it ends.
Avoiding parallax error: line your eye up directly with the scale marking. Looking at an angle makes the reading look bigger or smaller than it really is.

Measuring volume

For a regular solid (like a box), measure its length, width and height and calculate volume from those. For a liquid, use a measuring cylinder: keep it upright on a flat surface, and read the bottom of the curved liquid surface (the meniscus) at eye level.

Measuring time

The SI unit of time is the second (s). Use a stopwatch or digital timer for short intervals. To find one time interval from two stopwatch readings, subtract the earlier reading from the later one.

Why time several oscillations, not just one? Your reaction time when starting/stopping a stopwatch is a fixed error, roughly the same every time. If you only time one swing of a pendulum, that fixed error is a large fraction of the (short) time you measured. Time 20 swings instead, and the same fixed error becomes a tiny fraction of a much longer total time — so the percentage uncertainty in your answer drops a lot.

Finding a period by measuring multiples

Time a number of complete oscillations N (say, 20), then divide the total time t by N to get the period T of one oscillation.

Supplement (Extended only)

Scalars and vectors

A scalar quantity has size only. A vector quantity has size and a direction.

ScalarsVectors
distance, speed, time, mass, energy, temperature force, weight, velocity, acceleration, momentum, electric field strength, gravitational field strength

Combining two perpendicular vectors

When two vectors act at right angles to each other (for example, two forces, or a velocity and a current), you can find the size of their resultant in two ways:

  • By calculation: use Pythagoras' theorem, since the two vectors and their resultant form a right-angled triangle.
  • By scale drawing: draw the two vectors to scale, head-to-tail, then measure the straight line from the very start to the very end.

Equations

QuantityEquation
Period from repeated oscillationsT = t / N
Resultant of two perpendicular vectors x and y (Supplement)R = √(x² + y²)

7Key terms

Pronunciation reference: British Received Pronunciation (RP). /ə/ = the soft "uh" sound in an unstressed syllable (e.g. the "a" in "about") — this sound doesn't exist in Bahasa Indonesia, which is one reason these words feel tricky to say.

Lengthnoun
The distance from one point to another. SI unit: metre (m).
/leŋθ/"LENGTH" (rhymes with "strength")
🇮🇩panjang
Bahasa Indonesia has no "th" sound. Try touching your tongue lightly between your teeth for the "th" instead of saying "leng" or "lent".
"We measured the length of the desk with a ruler."
"Kami mengukur panjang meja itu dengan penggaris."
Volumenoun
The amount of space something occupies. SI unit: cubic metre (m³).
/ˈvɒljuːm/"VOL-yoom"
🇮🇩volume (serapan — borrowed word)
Don't say it like the Indonesian "volume" (/volume/). The English stress is on "VOL", and the ending is "-yoom", not "-um".
"The volume of the box is 200 cubic centimetres."
"Volume kotak itu adalah 200 sentimeter kubik."
Periodnoun
The time taken for one complete oscillation.
/ˈpɪəriəd/"PEER-ee-uhd"
🇮🇩periode
Stress the first syllable ("PEER-"), not the last, unlike the Indonesian "peri-ODE".
"The period of the pendulum was 1.8 seconds."
"Periode bandul itu adalah 1,8 detik."
Parallax errornoun phrase
A reading error caused by viewing a scale from an angle instead of straight on.
/ˈpærəlæks ˈerə/"PA-ruh-laks ERR-uh"
🇮🇩kesalahan paralaks
In British English the "r" at the end of "error" is very soft, almost silent — not a strong rolled "r" like in Indonesian.
"Reading the scale at an angle causes a parallax error."
"Membaca skala dari sudut kemiringan menyebabkan kesalahan paralaks."
Meniscusnoun
The curved surface of a liquid in a container; read at the bottom, at eye level.
/məˈnɪskəs/"muh-NIS-kuhs"
🇮🇩meniskus
Stress the middle syllable ("NIS"), not the first — it's "muh-NIS-kuhs", not "MEN-is-kus".
"Read the bottom of the meniscus at eye level."
"Bacalah bagian bawah meniskus sejajar dengan mata."
Scalarnoun / adjective
A quantity that has size (magnitude) only, with no direction.
/ˈskeɪlə/"SKAY-luh"
🇮🇩besaran skalar
The middle sound is a gliding "ay" as in "day", not a short "a" — and the ending is soft ("-luh"), not a hard "-lar".
"Speed is a scalar quantity because it has no direction."
"Kelajuan adalah besaran skalar karena tidak memiliki arah."
Vectornoun
A quantity that has both size and direction.
/ˈvektə/"VEK-tuh"
🇮🇩besaran vektor
The ending is soft ("-tuh"), not a hard, fully pronounced "-tor" like the Indonesian "vektor".
"Velocity is a vector quantity because it has both size and direction."
"Kecepatan adalah besaran vektor karena memiliki besar dan arah."
Resultantnoun
The single combined effect of two or more vectors acting together.
/rɪˈzʌltənt/"rih-ZUL-tuhnt"
🇮🇩resultan
Stress the second syllable ("ZUL"), and use the short "cup" vowel there, not "u" as in "put".
"The resultant of the two forces was 5000 newtons."
"Resultan dari kedua gaya tersebut adalah 5000 newton."
